# PARAGRAPHS
[1] (Heading 1) Executive Summary
[2] (First Paragraph) Phase 2 of the Eternal/Zomato valuation project focuses on rigorous data collection and validation. We first download all primary source documents (annual reports FY22–FY25, FY26 quarterly results, investor presentations, etc.) and archive them in the project folder. Next, we extract every needed financial line item and operating KPI into a “Master Extraction” table. Each figure is tagged with a source ID and URL. Then we cross-validate these numbers against secondary data (Moneycontrol, Screener, brokerage notes, etc.) to catch any discrepancies. Finally, we document checks to ensure consistency (sum of quarters equals annual, balance sheet tallies, IFRS vs “adjusted” figures, etc.). The attached tables and protocols give a complete blueprint for Phase 2A (Data Acquisition) and 2B (Data Verification & Entry).

[24] (Heading 1) 4. Reconciliation Protocol
[25] (First Paragraph) To ensure data integrity, we will perform these checks for every item:
[26] (Compact) Cross-check source vs source: For each number, verify it against at least two sources. For example, confirm Q3FY26 net profit (~₹102 Cr) using both the official letter and Business Standard【26†L42-L50】 or moneycontrol, to catch transcription errors.
[27] (Compact) Annual vs quarterly sums: Ensure sum of Q1–Q4 equals full-year FY26. (E.g. Q3+Q4+... = FY26). If not, re-examine e.g. one-offs or restatements.
[28] (Compact) Balance sheet balance: Check Total Assets = Total Liabilities + Equity in each year’s BS. If not, trace misaligned entries (like omitted share-based payments).
[29] (Compact) Cash flow reconciliation: Verify that CFO (Net income + adjustments) matches reported cash flow from operations in AR, and that capex matches investing cash flow.
[30] (Compact) Net Debt: Confirm Net Debt = Borrowings – Cash each year. If AR shows large cash, check that company is net cash. (Any discrepancy may come from lease liabilities – if so, treat separately or note IFRS 16).
[31] (Compact) Non-cash items & restatements: Watch for exceptional items, e.g. one-time bonus amortization or IndAS inventory rules. The Q3 letter noted a shift in “accounting for inventory in quick commerce”【45†L47-L54】; we must not double-count that in revenue. If a number seems off (e.g. higher “Adjusted Revenue” vs reported revenue), check management’s explanation.
[32] (Compact) Currency & Units: Ensure consistency (all ₹Crore unless noted). Double-check any foreign-currency items (Zomato has no material forex exposure).
[33] (Compact) Segment vs consolidated: When using segment NOV/GOV, confirm the sum across segments roughly matches total platform volume. (For example, Q3 B2C NOV from letters (25,732 Cr【45†L45-L48】) should equal Food+Blinkit NOV sum).
[34] (Compact) Shares outstanding: Verify share count from AR equity note vs number implied by EPS and PAT (EPS × shares = PAT). For instance, Q3 EPS=₹0.11【26†L42-L50】 and net profit=102 Cr implies ~927 Cr shares, close to 907 Cr listed in FY25 AR (differences likely from share issuances).
[35] (Compact) Formatting checks: Ensure no misplaced commas or percent signs, all numeric fields are numbers. Confirm that each raw-data cell uses the linked formula in Historical_FS or Operating_Model (per “Checks” tab of workbook).
[36] (Compact) Documentation: Flag any discrepancy >5% or unexplained item. For example, if Moneycontrol shows a different FY24 EBITDA margin, note the source and reason (bank might exclude hyperpure).
[37] (First Paragraph) If conflicts arise (e.g. Moneycontrol vs AR), we give precedence to company’s audited filings. We note any disagreements in the “Extraction notes.” For IFRS/IndAS differences, we’ll follow AR (IndAS) standards and record the “Adjusted” figures (e.g. Goyal’s “Adjusted Revenue”) as needed for valuation.
